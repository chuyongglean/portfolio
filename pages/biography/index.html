Let's Talk
My Journey

A Software Developer's Journey of Passion and Persistence

Born in the heart of Cameroon, my path to becoming a software developer was anything but ordinary. It's a story of resilience, passion, and unwavering dedication, forged through personal hardship and fueled by a desire to create. My full legal name is Chuyong Glean Ngang. My previous name was Shuyong Glean Ngang.

2005

Birth

Born on April 7th in Babungo, North-West Region of Cameroon.

2018

First Steps in Tech

Amidst the Anglophone crisis, I turned idleness into learning. I began building websites and exploring affiliate marketing, though this initial foray had its challenges.

2018-2021

Physical Trade & Resilience

Leaving tech temporarily, I immersed myself in physical trades, mastering welding and weaving. Weaving, in particular, became my primary income source for seven years, providing the means to acquire better tools.

2022 (Early)

Return to Education & Tech

Enrolled in BiRoCAST Complex, a new private school in my village. I juggled schooling with my passion for tech, focusing on Android app development using tools like App Creator 24, Modular, and Sketchware on my iTel A33.

2022-2023

Monetizing Apps & Community Building

Gained traction in AdMob groups, becoming known for selling apps to those who lacked coding skills. I built a Facebook group to 36k members, establishing an early online presence.

2023-2024

Blogging & Entrepreneurship

Founded 'Shuyong Tech' (initially 'App Admober'), focusing on online income, app creation, and ad networks. After initial struggles with content quality and AdSense approval, I relaunched successfully in 2024, later selling the blog and my Google Play Console to fund a new phone iTel A56.

2024

New Beginnings & Flutter

Acquired a new phone (iTel A56). Despite challenges with Play Console access, I learned Flutter and began building cross-platform applications. I continued to distribute Android versions via APK stores like Uptodown.

2024 (Mid)

Dropping Out & Dedication

Made the difficult decision to drop out of high school to fully commit to my dream of becoming a software developer, inspired by tech giants like Facebook.

2025 (April)

Embracing iOS & Web IDE

With a MacBook, I dove into iOS development, launching a quiz app ('Math Quiz: Learn Math Fast') and a notepad app ('Notables: Secured Notepad'). Inspired by my past struggles, I created 'Web IDE,' a simple app for landing page creation and web development, initially supporting just HTML, CSS, and JS.

2025 (Ongoing)

Growth and Impact

'Web IDE' evolved significantly, adding multi-file support, export, backup, and restore features. I continue to share my projects on platforms like Facebook, medium, whatsapp, google etc, striving to build solutions that empower others, especially those without access to laptops, just as I once was.

My journey, entirely self-taught through YouTube, without formal programming education, has been a testament to persistence. I've connected with supportive African developers along the way. My core mission remains: to build impactful solutions and empower aspiring developers worldwide.
